## Description

A subterranean necropolis carved into the limestone hills, once intended for the nobility of a forgotten city. Now, it serves as a feeding ground for restless spirits and scavenging horrors.

## History

Built six centuries ago by the Vorpal-Duke Alaric to house his lineage and their occult libraries. Alaric's obsession with avoiding death led him to experiment with soul-binding, eventually corrupting the entire site. The crypt was sealed by imperial decree when whispers from the vaults began driving the locals mad, leaving the Duke's riches and his research abandoned.

## Overarching Threat

The Ghost of Alaric, known as the Marrow-King, seeks a physical vessel to reclaim his title. His necrotic influence slowly drains the vitality of any living creature that stays too long, causing minor wounds to fester and heavy shadows to follow the party. He can sense anyone carrying gold from his vaults and will send restless spirits to retrieve it.

## Atmosphere

The air is freezing and carries a faint phosphor glow from lichen. Sounds of grinding stone and distant weeping echo through the halls. A cloying smell of old funeral incense and damp earth permeates the corridors.

## Rooms

- **The Threshold of Tears.** A cold stone vestibule where four distinct doors stand sentinel. Dust lies thick on the floor, disturbed only by the scratching of small vermin near the corners.
- **The Grinding Hall.** A narrow, oppressive corridor where the walls are lined with jagged iron spikes. The air is stagnant and carries a sharp smell of oxidized metal.
- **The Scribe's Ossuary.** Shelves carved into the walls contain skulls with runes etched into their foreheads instead of books. A low hum resonates from the center of the room.
- **Archivist's Nook.** This cramped space is filled with rotting scrolls and moldy parchment that crumble at the touch. The ceiling is sagging under the weight of the earth above.
- **The Pit of False Hope.** A dead-end chamber with a silver urn visible at the far end on a marble pedestal. The floor looks suspiciously clean compared to the rest of the crypt.
- **The Mourner's Walk.** Faded murals of funeral processions decorate the walls, their colors muted by time. This long room serves as a solemn approach to the master's hall.
- **The Gilded Sepulcher.** A lavishly decorated room featuring a gold-leafed sarcophagus in the center. The walls are inlaid with small, polished garnets that shimmer like watching eyes.
- **The Breath of Winter.** An unnaturally cold room where frost covers the stone floor and walls. The door at the far end is encrusted in thick, blue-tinged ice.
- **The Gravity Spike.** This vertical-focused chamber has odd, radial scorch marks on the ceiling. It feels as though the weight of the world is pressing down on your shoulders.
- **The Altar of the Pale Lord.** An altar made of fused human rib bones stands at the center of this shrine. Candles made of black wax burn with a flickering, green flame.
- **The Waystation.** A crossroads for the lower crypt where several paths meet. Chipped stone benches suggest this was a place for visitors to rest during ancient ceremonies.
- **The Marrow-King's Throne.** A vast hall dominated by a throne of jagged obsidian that seems to drink the light. The Ghost of Alaric hovers here, surrounded by the swirling spirits of his kin.
- **The Embalming Suite.** Stone tables are covered in rusted surgical tools and dried, blackened fluids. The smell of vinegar and ancient rot is overpowering here.
- **The Lesser Vault.** Numerous small alcoves hold the remains of distant relatives and lesser nobility. The bones are piled neatly, though some have clearly been chewed on.

## Wandering Monsters

- **Restless Spirit.** A wailing wraith-like figure that passes through walls to harass the party.
- **Grave Rats.** Small, aggressive rodents that attempt to steal rations.
- **Bone Golem Sentry.** A hulking construct made of fused femurs and ribs that patrols between depths.
