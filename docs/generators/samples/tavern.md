# The Hearth and Hemlock
## Lore

Legend says that the inn is built atop a fairy ring, and that on the summer solstice, the ale turns into nectar that can cure any minor ailment. Barnaby denies this, though he does charge double for rooms on that specific night.

## Quirks

The tavern's fireplace features a small stone face that occasionally sneezes sparks when someone tells a lie nearby. Additionally, the furniture is magically enchanted to subtly grow or shrink to perfectly accommodate the size of the creature sitting in it.

## Plot Hooks

- A regular patron, a local herbalist, has gone missing while searching for 'Silverleaf' in the nearby Whispering Woods.
- A tattered map was found tucked into the binding of a book in the reading nook, pointing to a forgotten cellar beneath the town square.
- The tavern's supply of Moon-Glow Ale has been hijacked by a band of goblins who are now reportedly throwing a massive party in a nearby cave.

## Menu

- **Honey-Glazed Roasted Carrots.** Tender root vegetables harvested from the back garden and roasted with wild thyme.
- **Hearty Venison Stew.** Chunks of slow-cooked deer meat with potatoes, leeks, and a thick brown gravy served in a bread bowl.
- **Forest Mushroom Tart.** A flaky pastry filled with creamed wild mushrooms and sharp goat cheese.
- **Brambleberry Cobbler.** Sweet and tart berries baked under a buttery, crumbly crust, served warm with a dollop of honey-cream.
- **Old Oak Cider.** A crisp, dry apple cider aged in charred oak barrels for a smoky finish.
- **The Hemlock Special.** A surprisingly non-toxic herbal tea blend that provides a gentle invigorating effect. Contains peppermint and ginger.
- **Spiced Mulled Wine.** Red wine simmered with cinnamon, cloves, and orange peel. Served in a heavy pewter mug.
- **Lavender Shortbread.** Light, floral biscuits that melt in the mouth. Often paired with evening tea.

## History

Originally an old watchtower from a forgotten border war, the building was converted into an inn fifty years ago. The thick stone walls provide excellent insulation and safety, making it a favorite stop for travelers during harsh winters.

## Patrons

- **Thistle of the High Forest** — Wood Elf Druid
- **Captain Valerius** — Retired Guard Captain
- **Kallista** — Traveling Mapmaker

## Intrigue

Barnaby is secretly a retired information broker for the Harpers. He keeps a ledger of every secret whispered in the private parlors, coded as recipes for different types of herbal tea.

## Amenities

- **The Sun-Drenched Reading Nook.** A quiet corner filled with overstuffed armchairs and a small library of local histories and botanical journals.
- **Cedar Wood Wash Tubs.** Private bathing stalls behind the hearth that utilize copper pipes to provide constant warm water.
- **The Verdant Stable.** A clean, hay-filled barn with a dedicated groomer who specializes in exotic mounts and pack animals.
- **Private Parlors.** Soundproofed small rooms available for rent by the hour for sensitive business negotiations.
