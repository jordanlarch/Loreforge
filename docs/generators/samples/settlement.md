## Description

Lowgate Cross sits where the Salt Way intersects the High Road, providing a rare dry passage through the treacherous Sinking Mire. The town rests on a massive slab of basalt that has never shifted or cracked in recorded memory. This unnatural stability allows heavy wagons to pass safely year-round. It exists because without this hub, the overland trade between the northern kingdoms and southern ports would simply vanish into the mud.
