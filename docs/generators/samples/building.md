# The Iron Paving Market
*Medium*

## Rumors

- If you pay with a coin minted the year of the fire, the ghosts will follow you home.
- One particular stall appears only on Tuesdays and sells bottled memories, but the price is a year of your life.
- The spirits are growing restless because someone stole the 'Key of the Gate' from the overseer's office.

## Secret

The Market Factor, Marlowe Graves, is actually a sentient undead using a Ring of Mind Shielding to hide his nature while he manages the spirits of his former colleagues.

## Building Type

Open-air Bazaar

## Description

A stone-paved square surrounded by tall, soot-stained buildings where the air remains unnaturally cold even in the height of summer. Merchants operate out of heavy iron-bound stalls or the backs of sturdy wagons, their goods laid out on salt-rimmed tables. A persistent thin mist clings to the ground, never rising above the knees of the shoppers.

## Atmosphere

Quiet and somber. Customers speak in hushed tones, and the usual boisterous shouting of a bazaar is replaced by the rhythmic clinking of silver charms and the unsettling sound of footsteps coming from empty alleys.

## Architectural Style

Gothic industrialism featuring heavy granite blocks, wrought-iron gates, and stalls reinforced with cold iron to deter spiritual interference.

## Notable Features

- The Weeping Fountain, a dry stone basin in the center that drips black ichor instead of water.
- Silver bells hanging from every stall lintel that chime when a non-living entity passes by.
- A massive, stationary clock tower that permanently points to midnight.
- Salt-lines drawn across every threshold and around every merchant's display.

## Condition

Haunted

## History

Three decades ago, the market was the site of a Great Fire that trapped hundreds of merchants and patrons behind a collapsed gate. Since that night, the market has been plagued by 'The Lingering,' spirits of the deceased who still try to conduct their daily business. The city council allowed the market to reopen only after hiring specialists to ward the stalls.

## Owner

- **Name:** Marlowe Graves
- **Role:** Market Factor
- **Personality:** A weary, no-nonsense bureaucrat who smells of sage and incense. He treats the hauntings as a logistical nuisance rather than a supernatural horror.

## Notable N P Cs

- **Kaelen the Shrewd.** A man with a nervous twitch who sells mundane supplies. He is rumored to have a spectral twin that stands behind him and points out thieves.
- **Old Maddy.** A blind tiefling who claims she can smell the history of an object. She specializes in items recovered from 'the other side'.
- **Brother Vane.** A scarred priest of a grave deity who patrols the aisles with a thurible to ensure the spirits don't become violent.

## Additional Sections

- **title:** Spectral Trade Goods, **content:** Aside from common gear, the market is known for 'Ghost-Touched' items. A level 5 party can find Potion of Etherealness (rarely), Oil of Sharpness, or silvered weapons. There is also a black market for 'Echo Crystals' which capture 10 seconds of sound from the past.
- **title:** Warding Regulations, **content:** All visitors must check their weapons at the gate, and each person is given a small sprig of dried rowan. Casting 'Turn Undead' within the market is a criminal offense as it causes a riot among the 'resident' spectral population.
- **title:** The Night Shift, **content:** After the sun sets, the living merchants depart and the market is officially 'closed.' However, those who stay can witness the 'Shadow Market' where entities from the Ethereal Plane trade in secrets and lost souls. Entering the Night Shift requires a specialized pass and carries a high risk of possession.
